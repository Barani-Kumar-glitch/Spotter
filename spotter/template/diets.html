PRO LEVEL GUIDANCE

Mastery & Intent

Tips

Focusing on Health

Prioritize Protein

Aim to include a protein source (eggs, lentils, chicken, tofu) in every meal. It keeps you full longer and protects your muscles.

Hydrate Before You Eat

Sometimes your brain confuses thirst with hunger. Drink a glass of water 20 minutes before a meal to clarify your appetite.

Start Small

Don't change everything on Monday. Pick one habit (like eating a vegetable with lunch) and master it before moving on.

Fiber is Your Friend

Aim for 25–30g of fiber daily. It aids digestion and regulates blood sugar levels.

The 80/20 Rule

Don't aim for perfection. Eat nutrient-dense foods 80% of the time, and allow yourself treats for the other 20%. This prevents the "binge-restrict" cycle.

Tricks

Convince Brain to Cheat Junk Food

The "Front-Loading" Trick

Eat your vegetables first, protein second, and carbs last. This slows down the glucose spike from the carbs and fills you up on low-calorie fiber first.

Freeze Your Fruit

Frozen grapes or berries take longer to eat and feel like a decadent dessert, making them a great alternative to ice cream.

The "H.A.L.T." Check

Before you reach for a snack, ask: Am I Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired? If it’s any of the last three, food won't fix it.

Use Smaller Plates

It sounds silly, but the "Delboeuf Illusion" is real. The same portion looks massive on a small plate and tiny on a large one, tricking your brain into feeling satisfied.

The "Two-Bite" Rule

For desserts, the first and second bites provide the most dopamine. Enjoy them slowly, then consider stopping. The tenth bite rarely tastes as good as the first.
